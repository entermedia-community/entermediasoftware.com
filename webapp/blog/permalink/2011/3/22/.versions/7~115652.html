California Dreaming on a Winters Day.

So . . . A little blog article about the EnterMedia trip out west.  I don't think any of us were truly looking forward to the connecting flights from Dayton International to LAX.  I watched the midwest pass plainly below on the first flight from the last row of the plane, which I had entirely to myself.  Something about Frontier airlines:  Our party wasn't seated together on any of the flights.  We had a brief lay-over in Denver, and then we headed over the Rockies, and to what we could only imagine lay beyond.  Only Jason, from JYS Productions knew for sure as this was his fifth LA trip this year.  After we landed we would find out that meant Roscoe's Chicken and Waffles all around, delicious!  So, sometime late Tuesday evening we arrived at LAX, which was undergoing fresh renovations.  We were glad to be off the plane, and we made our way to Enterprise car rental to pick up our ride.  Julie had reserved a mid-sized SUV, and we upgraded to a new Jeep, all black, with black tinted Windows (got tinted windows on my ride I'm inconspicuous - Outkast).  I volunteered to be designated driver, and we were off to the hotel.  We were all getting hungry, especially Cristopher, so we stopped at Roscoe's for what turned out to be some of the best food the whole trip.  Roscoe's is world famous for their Chicken and Waffles, and so we loaded up on food, and then we loaded into the car once more, and made for the hotel.

The Hilton at Universal city was a pretty nice four star.  We checked into our rooms on the 18th floor and Cristopher and Julie retired for the night.  Meanwhile Jason, and I managed to deploy some renegade wi-fi, and proceeded to take some iphone and DSLR footage of us chillin' at the top of the world . . . Hollywood!  After this quick dry run to make sure all of our portable technology was connected and fully operational, we hit the sack for an early convention the next day.

Jason was up, dressed to kill, and out the door first thing the next morning.  Julie, Cristopher, and he were off to the Create-a-sphere digital asset management convention.  Meanwhile,  I was charged with retrieving some brochures that were ready for pick up on Santa Monica Boulevard, and so I headed out for a magical little drive through Beverly Hills.

If you don't know, LA is the capitol of sprawl.  There's no good way to tell how long a trip around town is going to take you, but set aside about an hour for every twenty miles as the crow flies.

Ah.  The brochures.  Let's see.  There was typo, and it was IndiAdamJones's fault.  I was determined not to let it get to me.  I guess that's what happens when you make the final edits at the last minute with 10 kids in the house.  I should of caught it in the proof though, so I can't really blame that.  Okay, enough self-inflicted guilt.  Here's the brochure, they wanted me to correct it for the blog, but how is that interesting?  Here at EnterMedia we cut through political red-tape.  That's what makes us cool.

 This is where the brochure will be displayed along with PDF Download.

So . . . what happened next?  Got the brochures, right.   I slowly made my way back down Santa Monica Blvd in the Jeep.  There was a notion to stop at Staples, and get a sticker to cover the typo, and I made a quick stop.  Really though, nothing could undo the Spelling FAIL x 1,000 brochures.  Like I said, I'm letting it go.

Back at the ranch, Cristopher, Julie, and Jason were chopping it up with the who's who of digital asset management software.  Cristopher is the president of EnterMedia and a Java wizard.  He is soft spoken and an all around good guy, friendly to be around, and the father of four awesome kids.  You can be sure he was scoping out the competition's new features, and calculating how to implement them in his own master plan, ha, ha, ha.  To be sure, I took his name tag during the catered lunch spectacular, and masquereded incognito as Cristopher Burkey.  I sat down at a table labeled something like, "Weirdo's at a DAM convention," and proceeded to introduce myself, and MY company EnterMedia Software.  It was an easy sell.  I had them literally drooling over EnterMedia enterprise server platform (actually they were eating . . . that's why they were drooling, but anyway).  I met this nice lady who works for Ripley's Believe It or Not.  I finished my lunch and passed the tag back to Cristopher.
